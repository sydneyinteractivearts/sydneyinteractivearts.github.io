The Sydney Interactive Arts Meetup will be a place to talk interactive art, share ideas and projects, find collaborators, get feedback and support on your interactive art projects.

We welcome creatives / makers / artists / designers / musicians / creative coders / or anyone interested in the intersection of art and technology! We'll be hosting drinks, talks, workshops, show & tell sessions and more.

When is it?

Our inaugural meetup will be held in 2016, Thursday Jan 28th, 6pm. If you're interested in coming, or keeping up to date with our meetups,
please join our meetup group.

Where is it?

Stone & Chalk
Level 2, 50 Bridge St, Sydney



Stone & Chalk is a hub dedicated to supporting the growth of Fintech startups and currently has over 50 startup residents. The space is open and friendly — who knows what collaborations could foster between fintech and interactive artists? 😀

What can I expect?

  • Drinks and networking opportunities
  • Speaker talks ranging from project case studies and latest technologies, to how to write project proposals
  • Workshops on things like Arduino, creative coding, interaction design, etc
  • Show and Tell: present your project WIP as part of a series of lightning talks to get help, feedback, or find collaborators
  • Project time: Bring your project to the meetup and hack away with like-minded people. Begin a collaboration, get help solving a problem, or contribute to someone else's project!

Who will be speaking?

Our Thursday Jan 28th meetup has a theme of urban interactive art:

Social interaction design patterns for public media art
by Luke Hespanhol

Luke Hespanhol is a visual artist, interaction designer, software developer and lecturer of Urban Interaction Design at UNSW. In his talk he'll speak about his research and practice in urban interactive artworks.

From trash bin to award winning artwork
by Steven Bai & Sam Johnson

Steven Bai & Sam Johnson, creators of 'Tetrabin' (first exhibited at Vivid Light 2014), and winners of the Sydney Design Awards, will speak about taking their project from concept, through to promotion and award ceremonies.
